Deep winter had sealed the world, locking the promise of spring in a distant, unreachable future. The frost was not merely on the ground and in the trees; it was in the marrow. And while the turning of the earth guaranteed that spring must eventually conquer winter, to a starving animal, the 'eventual' is a poor substitue for 'now'.

Yet, against the odds, the animals had endured---even done great given the circumstance. The tragedy of Veritas's death had served as a grim wake-up call.

Yet, the animals had done well given the circumstances. Thanks to Veritas's dramatic death, the chipmunks had heavily rationed their food stores, and that of the Winter Fund. Through, Br'r Rabbit---and other trusted influencial animals---the message of low feed stores had been spread throughout the forest. It had been done subtly, very carefully, so as not to incite panic, and been effective enough to stop any real catastrophe in its tracks.

In the end, the animals were going to survive the winter, but it would still be the worst winter in living memory. And, as with any setback, there's plenty of blame to go around---whether justified or not at all.

Tensions that had been simmering under the winter snow finally---as it always does---erupted when a message of anger, frustration, and blame was smartly delivered by Br'r Rabbit with an act of public defiance.
